Why Water?

Water changes everything

Access to clean water can transform a community's health, education, and economy

Health

Clean water reduces waterborne diseases by 80% and protects children from early mortality.

Learn more

The Health Impact

Diseases from dirty water kill more people every year than all forms of violence, including war.

  • 443 million school days are lost each year due to water-related diseases
  • Women and children spend 40 billion hours collecting water each year
Read the research

Education

When clean water is available, children can go to school instead of spending hours fetching water.

Learn more

The Education Impact

With clean water nearby, children have time to go to school and get an education.

  • School attendance increases by up to 15% when clean water is available
  • For every year a girl stays in school, her income can increase by 10-20%
Read the research

Women Empowerment

Women and girls spend hours collecting water. Providing water gives them time for education and work.

Learn more

The Women's Impact

Women are responsible for 72% of water collection globally, often walking miles each day.

  • Women can start businesses with time saved from collecting water
  • Girls who don't have to collect water are 12% more likely to stay in school
Read the research

Economic Growth

Every dollar invested in water and sanitation projects returns $4 to the local economy.

Learn more

The Economic Impact

Access to clean water can transform a community's economic prospects.

  • The global economic return on water spending is $4.30 for every $1 invested
  • Water projects allow communities to diversify income through agriculture
Read the research

Stories

Lives transformed

Real stories of people whose lives have been changed by clean water

Sarah's Story

Ethiopia

Sarah used to walk 3 hours every day to collect water for her family. The water was often contaminated, making her siblings sick.

When charity: water built a well in her village, everything changed. Now Sarah attends school regularly and dreams of becoming a doctor.

"Water is life... Now I have time to study and dream of a better future. My family no longer gets sick from contaminated water."
Read full story

James's Story

Uganda

James's community relied on a contaminated stream for water. Waterborne diseases were common, and many children missed school due to illness.

After a charity: water project brought clean water to their village, school attendance increased by 80%, and James started a small garden with the extra time he had.

"Clean water has given us hope. Our children are healthier, and we can focus on building a better future."
Read full story

Maria's Story

Bolivia

Maria and her daughters spent over 20 hours per week collecting water from a distant source. This left little time for education or income-generating activities.

With clean water now available in their village, Maria has started a small business selling handcrafted goods, and her daughters are top students in their class.

"Water is more than just something to drink. It's time, it's opportunity, it's a future for my daughters."
